# Phase 2 — Tournament Design

Goal: turn ~20-30 raw pain candidates from Phase 1 into one winning business concept, chosen by structured competition rather than my first instinct.

## Stage A — Shortlist (orchestrator)
Merge the four dossiers, dedupe overlapping pains, and score every candidate 1–5 on:
- **Evidence strength** — number & quality of verified quotes, recency
- **Pain intensity** — emotional language, money/hours quantified
- **Willingness to pay** — existing paid tools, stated pay intent, incumbent pricing
- **Underserved-ness** — do current tools demonstrably fail (verified complaints about them)?
- **Buildability this month** — can a credible v1 be built locally by one agent, no new spend?
- **Reachable market** — can you find the buyers where they already congregate?

Top 4 candidates advance.

## Stage B — Founder pitches (4 parallel agents)
Each finalist pain gets an independent "founder" agent that must design the best possible business around it: sharpest wedge, target persona, product shape, pricing, go-to-market, moat, and a 90-day plan. Founders may do additional verification research (competitor pricing pages, market size anchors) — same URL-evidence rules. Output: one pitch memo each.

## Stage C — Judge panel (3 parallel agents)
Three judges with deliberately different lenses score all 4 pitches independently (1–10 per criterion, forced ranking, no ties for #1):
1. **The VC** — market size, wedge-to-expansion story, moat, why-now.
2. **The bootstrapper** — time-to-first-dollar, CAC realism, churn risk, solo-operability.
3. **The target customer** — would the actual persona adopt it? switching costs, trust, workflow fit.

## Stage D — Skeptic kill-shot (1 agent)
The provisional winner (highest combined forced-rank score) gets a dedicated skeptic whose only job is to refute it: verify the core evidence still holds, hunt for existing products that already solve it, find why the market is smaller or the pain shallower than claimed. Every kill-claim needs a URL too.

Decision rule: if the skeptic lands a fatal, evidence-backed blow (e.g., a well-funded incumbent already does exactly this, or the core quotes misread), the runner-up is promoted and gets the same skeptic treatment. Otherwise the winner stands, and the skeptic's surviving objections become the risk register for Phase 3.

Tie-break rule: if judge panel scores are within 5%, the bootstrapper lens wins (this must be takeable to market this month by one person, per the mission).
