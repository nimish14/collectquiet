## Executive Summary

One- and two-star reviews across G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot reveal a repeating pattern: **buyers pay premium subscription fees for category-leading tools, then get trapped by lock-in, broken integrations, or support that vanishes after the contract is signed.** The loudest pain clusters are:

1. **Post-sale support collapse** — AI chatbots, no phone access, multi-day silence while money-critical workflows break (AppFolio, Calendly, Acuity, Rent Manager).
2. **Accounting/integration lies** — “Syncs with QuickBooks” that fails on refunds/changes; walled gardens that force duplicate entry (Jobber, Bench, Emburse Nexonia).
3. **Pricing bait-and-switch** — Free months, add-on upsells, doubled back-half contract costs, withheld deposits (ServiceTitan, AppFolio, Calendly).

These are not free-tier complainers. Reviewers describe **active paid subscriptions**, multi-year contracts, and real business harm (late fees, tax overpayment, lost bookings, eviction threats).

## Top 3 Pain Candidates (ranked for company-building)

### 1. P1 — Bench: paying for books that never arrive
**Why #1:** Direct monetary harm (overpaid taxes), multi-year deliverable gap while subscription continues, emotional “almost ruined my business” language. Clear gap for a product that **guarantees close deadlines** or **integrates with existing invoicing/expense stack** instead of a walled garden.

### 2. P4 — AppFolio: contract trap + support vacuum
**Why #2:** B2B SaaS classic — great sales motion, 2-year lock-in, then tenants can't pay rent and PMs can't reach humans. High ACV property management market with **switching friction** creates room for a challenger that sells **month-to-month + phone support SLAs**.

### 3. P2 — ServiceTitan: flagship FSM price with import disasters & add-on bait-and-switch
**Why #3:** 0/5 review describes catastrophic data migration (lost/duplicated customers) plus promotional pricing reversal on Market Pro. Field service owners already pay five-figure stacks; pain is **implementation risk + add-on nickel-and-diming**, not lack of features.
